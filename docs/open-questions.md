# Open Design Questions

- What does the interestingness signal weight each input? Needs tuning through play.
- What needs exist at simulation start and how do they scale with delta-time?
- What thresholds trigger narrative-worthy LLM calls vs silent simulation?
- What does a thriving vs failing civilization look like — is there a win/lose condition or is it pure sandbox?
- What happens when the player ignores prayers consistently — faith collapse, behavioral change, institutional drift?
- How is time compression communicated in the UI without breaking immersion?
- What is the minimum viable capability graph to start — what nodes exist at simulation genesis?
- What tile topology should the world use — square or hexagonal? Hex grids give uniform adjacency distance (6 equidistant neighbors vs. 4 cardinal + 4 diagonal at different distances for square), which matters for movement costs, spread mechanics, and perceived fairness. The state model is currently topology-agnostic (`Tile` has no coordinate fields; tiles are a flat `BTreeMap<TileId, Tile>`), so the decision is deferred until adjacency is needed. That decision point is whenever movement or spread is introduced.
- **How do civilizations merge and split?** The continuous metric space supports emergent fragmentation and absorption (low `cohesion` + low `leadership_concentration` + high `resource_pressure` crossing thresholds; conquest from high aggression + capability differential), but the mechanical handling is unspecified. Open sub-questions: (1) When a split occurs, how are two new `Civilization`+`Cohort` pairs created — how is population, capability profile, and belief profile divided? (2) When one civ absorbs another, does the absorbed `Civilization` record get retired, and is there a `CivArchive` equivalent for dead civilizations analogous to `AgentArchive`? (3) How do orphaned `CivRelation` entries resolve after a merger? (4) If the focus civ splits, which half does `focus_civ_id` follow — does the player choose? (5) Should `AgreementType` include a `Federation` variant for partial mergers where both entities retain distinct identity? The philosophy is clearly emergent-over-designed; the gap is purely in the state mutation rules for these events.
- What is the right adjacency representation for large worlds? A naive `BTreeMap<TileId, Vec<TileId>>` stored in `PhysicalWorld` works for small grids but does not scale — a 1000×1000 hex world has 1 million tiles and 6 million neighbor entries to iterate during spread and movement steps. Crucially, adjacency is **not static**: divine intervention can raise mountains or flood valleys, and player-unlocked capabilities can construct bridges or roads — all of which change which tiles are traversable and at what cost. Any representation must support mutation mid-simulation, ruling out purely implicit coordinate arithmetic (which assumes a regular grid with no local modifications). Alternatives worth evaluating: a `BTreeMap<TileId, Vec<TileId>>` edge list (simple, mutable, poor cache locality at scale); a mutable CSR rebuilt on terrain change (cache-friendly iteration, O(n) rebuild cost per change — acceptable if changes are rare); or a layered model where base topology is implicit from coordinates and a sparse overlay records modifications (bridges, blocked passes). The choice should be resolved before movement or disease spread is implemented.
